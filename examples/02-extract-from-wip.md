# Example — Appraise, work in progress

Usage: *see what leverage you can pull out of something you're already doing.*

## Input

> "I've been refactoring my personal task tool for two weeks. Just a cleanup —
> better data model, fewer moving parts. Is there leverage here or is it just
> chores?"
> Status: **ongoing**

## Read

**RAISE · Substance 4/6 · Signal 1/4 · sweet-spot: no**

- **Substance (latent external value):** pain_density 1 (over-complex personal
  tooling is a moderately common pain), frequency 2 (simplification — "fewer
  moving parts, simpler data model" — is a *pervasive* engineering pattern many
  people hit), leverage 1 (the design *lesson* could travel; the refactor itself
  is yours, so scored down). Sum 4 — clears the gate. The pattern is broadly
  relevant even though the artifact is personal.
- **Signal (legibility):** only `self_starting` is surfaced. `named_tradeoff` is
  *latent* — a refactor is nothing but tradeoffs ("fewer moving parts" = you
  traded X for Y), but you haven't articulated it, so it doesn't count (latent ≠
  counted). `frontier_tool_use` and `future_state` are absent as described.
  Signal **1/4**.
- **Sweet spot:** no — restructuring a tool's internals isn't "clean form to
  unstructured data." (Don't force the lane where it doesn't fit.)

So as described this is a **Raise**: real substance, invisible. All the leverage
is latent in the framing.

**Binding constraint:** the value is real but buried. "I did a cleanup" is
invisible; the `named_tradeoff` is the load-bearing lift — it's *right there* in
the work, just unspoken.

**Move — Raise → reframe (watch the tier climb):** zero extra building. Articulate
the one design decision you're proudest of — *"I cut my task tool's data model in
half: here's the tradeoff, and why simpler won."* That surfaces `named_tradeoff`,
taking Signal to 2/4 → **Borderline**; lead with the lesson others can reuse and
it reads **Post**. The score moves with what you *show*, not what you did.
